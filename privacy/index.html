Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 4, 2026

Playdate ("we", "us", or "the app") helps you make plans with friends by combining your calendar availability with theirs. This policy explains what information Playdate collects, how it is used, and the choices you have. The short version: we collect only what the features you use require, we share free/busy status — never your event details — with the friends you choose, we don't show ads, and we never sell your data.

1. Information we collect

Account information

When you sign in with Google or Apple, we receive your name, email address, and profile photo (if available) to create your account.

Calendar data

If you choose to connect a calendar:

  • Google Calendar — we read your free/busy times and the events on calendars you select, and (when you use those features) create or update calendar entries for plans you make in Playdate, such as RSVPs, tentative holds while a time poll is open, and confirmed hangouts.
  • Outlook Calendar — we read your free/busy times to compute shared availability.
  • Apple Calendar — accessed on your device only; Apple Calendar data is never transmitted to our servers.

Calendar refresh tokens are stored encrypted on our backend and used only to perform the calendar operations described above on your behalf.

Google API Limited Use disclosure: Playdate's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Google Calendar data is used only to provide user-facing scheduling features, is never used for advertising, and is never sold.

Availability preferences

Your "hangout hours" (preferred days and times, minimum notice, weekly limits) are stored so Playdate can filter when plans get suggested. These preferences are never shown to other users — friends only ever see the resulting free or busy status.

Social and planning content

Content you create in the app: your friend (playmate) connections, circles, events, RSVPs, comments, poll votes, standing hangout preferences, booking links, and post-event feedback.

Location

With your permission, we use your device's approximate location to suggest nearby venues for plans. Location is used at the time of the request and is not tracked in the background or stored as a location history.

Device information

A push notification token for your device (so we can send you invitations, poll reminders, and plan updates) and basic diagnostic logs when errors occur.

2. How we use information

  • Compute when you and your friends are mutually free.
  • Create, update, and clean up calendar entries for plans you make.
  • Propose hangouts you've asked for (standing hangouts, booking links).
  • Send notifications about invitations, polls, and plan changes.
  • Suggest venues and activities relevant to your plans.
  • Maintain the security and reliability of the service.

We do not show ads, and we do not sell or rent your personal information.

3. What other users can see

Sharing in Playdate is deliberate and tiered:

  • Friends you've connected with see your free/busy status only — never event titles, descriptions, locations, or attendees from your calendar.
  • You control visibility per friend group ("circle") with permission tiers; lower tiers see less or nothing.
  • Event details in Playdate are visible only to people you invite, or at the reduced level your sharing tier allows.
  • If you share a booking link, people with the link see bookable time slots — computed from your availability — but never your underlying calendar.

4. AI features

The Schedule assistant sends your scheduling request, your availability windows, and your saved activity preferences to Anthropic's Claude API to generate suggestions. This data is processed to answer your request and is not used by Anthropic to train models. AI requests are rate-limited and only ever initiated by you.

5. Service providers

We rely on a small set of processors to run Playdate:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and backend hosting.
  • Google — sign-in, Calendar API, and Places API (venue search).
  • Microsoft — Outlook calendar free/busy (if connected).
  • Apple — Sign in with Apple (if used).
  • Anthropic — AI scheduling suggestions.
  • Expo — push notification delivery.

Each provider processes data only as needed to provide their service to us.

6. Data retention and deletion

We keep your data while your account is active. You can disconnect a calendar at any time, which stops all access to it; stored tokens for that calendar are removed.

To delete your account and associated data, contact us at the address below. We will delete your account data within 30 days, except where we are legally required to retain it. Calendar entries Playdate created on your own calendar remain yours and are not deleted from your calendar.

7. Security

All data is transmitted over HTTPS. Calendar tokens are stored in an encrypted vault accessible only to backend services. Database access is protected by row-level security so users can only reach data they're authorized to see. On your device, session and calendar tokens are stored in the operating system's secure storage.

8. Children

Playdate is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

9. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information, and to object to or restrict certain processing. You can exercise these rights by contacting us. We do not discriminate against you for exercising them.

10. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we'll update the effective date above and notify you in the app before the changes take effect.

11. Contact

Questions, requests, or complaints: privacy@playdate.example
